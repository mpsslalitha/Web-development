Time Management

Timeboxing is a goal-oriented time management strategy where you complete work within “timeboxes.”

Time blocking is similar to timeboxing, but instead of scheduling specific time for each individual task, you’ll practice blocking off set periods of your calendar for related work.

The Pomodoro time management strategy is particularly helpful because it actively encourages regular breaks, which are good for intrinsic motivation—and good for your brain.

The Eat the Frog time management strategy takes inspiration from this quote and encourages you to tackle big or complex tasks first before working on your less important or less urgent work.

The Pareto principle has one fundamental rule: you spend 20% of your time on 80% of your work.
